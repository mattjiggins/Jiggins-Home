CPD Website Redesign

Initial Presentation

The website had to be responsive. To accomplish this I tried an Atomic Design approach. Presenting the client with pieces of what might be on the website, in horizontal rather than vertical format. This helped focus the client on content pieces.

During discussions with the CPD communications department it became clear that there were many different and competing sections within the organization. Each section had a slightly different goal or objective. However, ultimately the organization is about "educational events". There are two distinct groups: Organizers (Educators) and Attendees (Doctors and Other Health Professionals).

My thought was each group needs its own "action word". Educators want to Educate and Attendees want to Learn.

The third primary section within the department was Research. This was a strong enough word to remain as it was.

Finally, after some debate, and to fit with their separately discovered "tag" the fourth action word was Transform.

A Mockup

Now we have our "Action" words - do we need to explain them? The initial design mockup showed 6 action words with some explainations in each box. The idea of matching Colour and Action also starts to be finalized. Red (strongest) represents the Learn or Event Registration.

At this stage a basic HTML, SASS framework is created. Design comes out of Photoshop and starts in the Browser.

However, presenting the client a design inside a computer seems to greatly enhance acceptance.

The Options

Time to present the client with three options. Option One is the revised version of the initial mockup. Option Two is a client proposed option to "move the events listing up to the top". Option Three is a radical re-design of the action words.

Option Three goes to full development.

The Build

Initial build will be in WordPress alone, however the live site presented a major obsticle: Event listing must occur on the homepage. In the past a simple link to their Event Management System was used.

To do this I created a simple read-only JSON REST Api to their existing MySQL data. Their event management system was written in Ruby. SlimFramework was perfect for this task.

Initally the code was written in basic JS/jQuery. Recently BackboneJS has been used to cleanup and simplify the events search listing.
